Investigating nonnegativity

Let $p=a_1 G_1 + a_2 G_2 + \ldots + a_k G_k$ be a linear combination of unlabeled graphs.

[Lov√°sz, Szegedy 2009]: If $p\geq 0$, then $p + \varepsilon$ is a SOS for any $\varepsilon > 0$.
[Hatami, Norin, 2011]: The question
"Does $p \geq 0$ hold?"
is undecidable.

Applications of flag algebras: Directed graphs

Caccetta-Haggkvist conjecture: Every simple directed graph on $n$ vertices with minimum outdegree $\frac{n}{3}$ contains a directed triangle.

Point order types

Hill's conjecture: The crossing number of $K_n$ is $$\mathrm{cr}(K_n) =\frac{1}{4}\left\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \right\rfloor \left\lfloor \frac{n-1}{2} \right\rfloor \left\lfloor \frac{n-2}{2} \right\rfloor \left\lfloor \frac{n-3}{2} \right\rfloor$$

[Balogh, Lidicky, Salazar 2019]: The conjecture is at least $98.5\%$ true.
